Brass Instruments

Brass instruments have coned-shaped tubes that are often made of brass. They are played with a mouthpeice and sound is produced buy vibrating the lips while blowing. You can control the pitch of a note by either tightening(for high notes) or loosening(for low notes) the lips. Some brass instruments have either finger valves or a slider like in a trombone to produce various notes. Some notable brass instruments include: trumpet, trombone, tuba, and french horn. Learn more here.

Woodwind Instruments

Most Woodwind instruments are blown on one end using finger holes to produce different notes. Most are blown from a reed or blowhole by vibrating against it while blowing air. How short or long the air flow is in the column controls how high or deep a note is played. Some notable woodwind instruments include: flute, clarinet, and saxophone. learn more about woodwind instruments here.

String Instruments

String instruments develop sound by vibrating the strings. Variation in sound depending on how loose or tight the strings are. Long and thicker strings produce deeper sound than shorter and thinner ones. Some notable string instruments are: harp, violin, and variations of guitars. Learn more about them here.

Percussion Instruments

They are some of the simplest and most primitive form of instruments. They vary from different shapes, forms, and the way they are played. Drums for example, are played by hitting the head and vary in sound based on its shape and how hollow it is. Some instruments are shaken in the case of maracas and bells. Pianos are played using the keys and xylophones are played by hitting the keys with a stick. Learn more about percussion instruments here.
